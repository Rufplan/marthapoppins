Our Philosophy

A different kind
of daycare

At Martha Poppins' Childcare & Art Academy, a Family Childcare Home, we believe in a holistic approach to early childhood education, nurturing the physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual wellbeing of every child. These four dimensions work together — what affects one influences the others — so our care embraces them all through art, nature, music, play, and mindful cooking.

🌱
Physical — We promote healthy growth through movement, balanced nutrition, proper rest, and positive habits.
💛
Emotional — We help children understand, express, and regulate their emotions, developing empathy and soft skills from an early age.
✨
Mental — We create stimulating learning experiences that encourage curiosity, problem-solving, and the joy of acquiring new skills.
🕊️
Spiritual — We foster a sense of purpose, gratitude, and connection with life, encouraging mindfulness and respect for values that guide positive actions.

Our methodology combines heart-centered guidance with immersive, play-based learning, where boundaries are seen as bridges, not walls. We invite children to explore aromas, colors, and melodies through art, cooking, and nature — building confidence, empathy, and a lifelong love for learning.

"Every aroma, color, and melody is a seed we plant in a child's soul."
— Martha Kerr, Founder

Our Vision

Heart-Centered
Guidance

At Martha Poppins' Childcare & Art Academy, we believe boundaries are bridges, not walls — gentle guides that help children feel safe, respect themselves, and live in harmony with others.

Our Boundaries Philosophy
Provide Safety: "I keep you safe so you can explore."
Encourage Independence: "You can try on your own, and I'm here if you need help."
Foster Empathy: "We take turns so everyone feels included."
Build Self-Awareness: "I notice you're feeling upset; let's find a calm way to solve this."

We lead with love, respect, and predictable routines, focusing on connection before correction and nurturing emotional intelligence. In this way, each child grows with confidence, empathy, and resilience in an environment that is artistic, nature-inspired, and safe.

"Where art, nature, and kindness shape little hearts."
🌱

Our Mission

At Martha Poppins Childcare & Art Academy, our mission is to nurture the body, mind, heart, and spirit of every child through conscious, artistic, and heartfelt care.

We believe that childhood is a sacred stage of life, where creativity and a deep connection to nature become the foundations for lifelong growth. We provide a safe, welcoming, and aesthetically inspiring environment where each child can explore, learn, and express themselves freely.

Our program integrates naïve art, music, theater, wholesome cooking, photography, mindful movement, and sensory experiences — cultivating not only academic and motor skills, but also self-esteem, empathy, and resilience.

Guided by the principles of holistic education, we understand that true learning flourishes when the mind is curious, the heart is open, the body is active, and the spirit feels at home. At Martha Poppins, every day is an opportunity to plant seeds of creativity, self-love, and respect for the world around us.

Core Learning Areas

How Children Grow &
Discover With Us

🎨
A — Art
Naïf Art & Nature Inspiration
  • Create art inspired by Golden Gate Park visits, garden time, and group activities.
  • Use natural materials collected on walks — leaves, sticks, flowers, stones — for collages and sculptures.
  • Learn about Latin American and world artists connected to nature and community: Diego Rivera, Rufino Tamayo, Frida Kahlo, Xul Solar.
  • Encourage free expression and creativity as a foundation for self-esteem.
🎶
B — Music
Rhythm, Piano & Nature Sounds
  • Practice rhythm by imitating sounds of nature: clapping like bird calls, percussion with sticks, seeds, or water.
  • Learn songs about animals, seasons, weather, and plants in Spanish and English.
  • Explore piano and soft classical music to support transitions and calming moments.
  • Perform small "mini concerts" for peers or during outdoor playtime in the park.
🗣️
C — Spanish Immersion
Living Language through Nature
  • All greetings, instructions, and songs are conducted in Spanish, supported by visuals and gestures.
  • Vocabulary connected to park destinations: pez (fish) at the pond, árbol (tree) on nature walks, pájaro (bird) in the garden.
  • Spanish storytelling during snack or picnic time in the park.
  • Role-play games and traditional Latin American children's songs.
💛
D — Social–Emotional Skills
Community & Gratitude
  • Partner and group challenges in playground settings to foster cooperation.
  • Practice sharing art materials and turn-taking — "my turn / your turn."
  • Daily gratitude and reflection circles at the start and close of the day.
  • Conflict resolution with simple Spanish phrases: "quiero compartir" / I want to share, "necesito ayuda" / I need help.
  • Daily reinforcement of self-esteem: positive affirmations and celebrating each child's unique talents.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What ages do you accept?
+
We welcome children from 3 months to 4 years old into our boutique program.
How many children are in the program?
+
We maintain a maximum of six children at all times. This allows for truly personalized attention, deep relationships and a calm learning environment where every child is truly seen.
What are your hours?
+
We are open Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Please provide advance notice for any mid-day pick-ups to avoid interrupting nap time.
Are meals included in tuition?
+
Yes! All meals and snacks are included. We prepare fresh, organic, homemade food daily — no processed foods or refined sugar. We accommodate all dietary needs and allergies. Children also participate in weekly cooking classes.
Do children need to know Spanish?
+
No prior Spanish knowledge is needed! The Martha Poppins Method introduces Spanish naturally through music, storytelling, art and daily interaction — the same way children learned their first language.
How do I enroll or join the waitlist?
+
Due to our small boutique group, spaces are very limited. Call or text (415) 917-6137 or fill out the contact form on this page and we'll be in touch within 24 hours.

I was born in Valencia, Venezuela, into a family deeply connected to art, music, and natural wisdom. I grew up surrounded by the scents of spices, classical music, and conversations about healing and creativity.

My father, a gifted pianist, passed on his love for music from a very early age. By four, I was already playing Beethoven melodies. Creativity was part of our daily life — painting, literature, healthy cooking, and body expression shaped my childhood.

At 23, I left Venezuela to explore cultures, landscapes, and diverse philosophies. I became a diplomatic lady, representing Mexico abroad. My final post was in Cuba, where I studied art history, philosophy, yoga, and theater with extraordinary Cuban masters.

Nine years ago, I arrived in San Francisco — a city that embraced me with its diversity, nature, and free spirit. The birth of my children sparked the creation of Martha Poppins Childcare & Art Academy, an intimate, conscious, creative space. Every child is an artist, a philosopher in the making, a sensitive soul who deserves to be guided with respect, inspiration, and beauty.

Born in La Libertad, El Salvador, Glendi grew up surrounded by nature, tradition, and community. She is a proud mother of two children, who are her greatest source of inspiration and strength.

With a special talent for cooking, Glendi has mastered the art of preparing pupusas entirely from scratch — a traditional Salvadoran dish she makes with dedication and love.

In San Francisco, Glendi has gained solid experience in early childhood education. She has completed all required trainings including CPR and First Aid certification, child abuse prevention, child safety, and all mandatory childcare health and safety courses.

Her patience and wisdom gently support children in learning Spanish as a second language, enriching every child with deeper cultural richness and kindness.

Mayte Teresa is a Venezuelan multidisciplinary artist with over 20 years of experience in theater, education, and creative facilitation. Trained at the National Theater Company and Workshop of Venezuela, she has performed in more than 30 productions with renowned companies including Rajatabla and Xiomara Moreno Productions.

As part of the Martha Poppins program, Mayte leads Corporal Kids — guided body expression classes in natural settings including parks and forests. Her sessions integrate dance, creative movement, and sensory exploration to foster confidence, self-expression, and a joyful connection to nature.

Mayte also leads Naif painting workshops, craft activities, and artistic storytelling. With a gentle, child-centered approach, she nurtures curiosity, empathy, and self-confidence.

Edgar Rey was born in Valencia, Venezuela. He came to the United States to continue his musical legacy, learning from new cultures and leaving a vibrant Latin mark on every student he works with. His teaching style blends Caribbean warmth with deep respect for every musical genre.

Edgar started formal piano studies at age 13 with maestro Gabriel Otazo — a lifelong mentor and family friend. He has performed at international events including the UN Climate Change Conference (COP16) in Cancun, Mexico.

He currently serves as music coordinator at a Catholic church in San Francisco while also teaching music to children in schools and privately. He is pursuing certifications at City College of San Francisco and training in pipe organ performance.
